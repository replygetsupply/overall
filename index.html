Daycare Whitepaper

Executive Summary

Daycare creates a decentralized network of childcare facilities through strategic acquisition of undervalued properties. By leveraging creator fees to fund facility leases at unprecedented rates, we build a self-sustaining ecosystem of community-owned childcare infrastructure.

The Opportunity

The US Government has begun divesting daycare properties in Minnesota, offering lease agreements at extraordinarily low rates. Buildings that traditionally command $3,000-5,000 monthly are now available for just $500 per month through government surplus property programs.

Our Solution

Daycare systematically leases these facilities using revenue generated from creator fees on our platform. Each successful lease expands our network, creating more capacity for community-driven childcare services while generating sustainable income streams.

Revenue Model

Platform creator fees fund facility acquisitions. Leased buildings generate revenue through childcare services, which in turn funds additional property leases. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle of expansion and community benefit.

Market Strategy

We focus on Minnesota properties being divested through US Government surplus programs. Our legal team ensures all lease agreements meet federal and state compliance standards, with particular attention to GSA property disposition requirements.

The Flywheel Effect

Each leased facility generates operational revenue that funds more leases. More facilities increase our market coverage and community impact. Greater coverage generates more platform activity and creator fees. This self-reinforcing cycle creates systematic expansion of affordable childcare infrastructure.

Legal Framework

All leases comply with GSA standards and federal property regulations. Our acquisition strategy focuses on legally documented lease agreements with clear terms, liability coverage, and community benefit provisions.
